Faith Hill Community Analysis

Visualizing cultural identity through data

Timeline 10 weeks, Spring 2024
Role Researcher, Data Designer
Course Data Visualization, Parsons
Tools D3.js, Python, Figma, Observable
Community Data Visualization

Overview

An analytical exploration combining cultural research with data visualization to understand community dynamics and identity formation, demonstrating how quantitative data can reveal nuanced cultural patterns when combined with thoughtful design.

This project emerged from an interest in how data visualization can tell human stories. Faith Hill—a historic neighborhood—has undergone significant demographic and cultural changes over the past decades. The challenge was to visualize these transformations in a way that respected the community's narrative while revealing patterns invisible in raw statistics.

The work balances analytical rigor with empathetic storytelling, showing how design can bridge quantitative data and lived human experience.

Research Question

How can data visualization illuminate cultural identity and community change while maintaining narrative authenticity and context?

Research Methodology

Data Collection

I gathered data from multiple sources to build a comprehensive picture:

  • Census Data: Demographic information spanning 30 years (1990-2020)
  • Historical Archives: Newspaper articles, photographs, city planning documents
  • Community Interviews: Conversations with 15 long-time residents
  • Business Records: Changes in local businesses and institutions
  • Social Media Analysis: Geotagged posts revealing activity patterns

Data Cleaning & Processing

Raw data required significant transformation before visualization:

  • Standardizing geographic boundaries across different census years
  • Handling missing data and interpolation challenges
  • Geocoding historical addresses to modern coordinate systems
  • Text analysis and topic modeling of qualitative interviews

Ethical Considerations

Working with community data required careful ethical deliberation:

  • Ensuring individual privacy while showing aggregate patterns
  • Presenting data in context to avoid misinterpretation
  • Consulting community members throughout the process
  • Acknowledging the limitations and biases in available data

Visual Analysis & Insights

Key Findings

The visualization process revealed several significant patterns:

01

Demographic Shifts

Significant changes in age distribution and ethnic composition, with younger populations replacing aging residents

02

Economic Transformation

Rising property values correlating with business turnover, affecting community stability

03

Cultural Continuity

Despite physical changes, certain cultural institutions maintained community identity

04

Social Networks

Analysis revealed how community bonds shifted from place-based to interest-based connections

Visualization Approach

I developed multiple visualization types, each highlighting different aspects of the data:

  • Timeline visualization: Showing demographic changes over 30 years with contextual annotations
  • Geographic heat maps: Revealing spatial patterns of change across the neighborhood
  • Network diagrams: Illustrating relationships between community institutions
  • Comparative charts: Contextualizing Faith Hill within broader city trends
  • Narrative scrollytelling: Combining data with resident quotes and historical photos

Design Decisions

  • Color palette: Warm, earthy tones reflecting community character rather than clinical data aesthetics
  • Typography: Accessible fonts balancing readability with personality
  • Interaction patterns: Progressive disclosure allowing both overview and detailed exploration
  • Responsive design: Ensuring visualizations work across devices while maintaining clarity

Accessibility

All visualizations were designed with accessibility in mind:

  • WCAG AA color contrast compliance
  • Alternative text descriptions for all graphics
  • Keyboard navigation support
  • Data tables accompanying visual representations

Reflection & Learnings

What Worked Well

  • Community engagement: Regular feedback from residents ensured visualizations resonated authentically
  • Multi-modal storytelling: Combining quantitative data with qualitative narratives created richer understanding
  • Iterative refinement: Multiple rounds of prototyping improved clarity and impact
  • Context preservation: Careful annotation prevented data from being misinterpreted in isolation

Challenges Faced

  • Data gaps: Historical data was incomplete, requiring thoughtful handling of uncertainty
  • Complexity vs. clarity: Balancing analytical depth with accessible presentation
  • Objectivity concerns: Ensuring personal perspective didn't overshadow community voices
  • Technical limitations: Performance issues with large datasets required optimization

Key Takeaways

Data needs context: Numbers alone can mislead. The most meaningful visualizations emerged when quantitative data was paired with qualitative understanding of what those numbers represented in people's lives.

Design is responsibility: Visualizing community data isn't neutral—choices about what to show, how to show it, and what to emphasize shape narratives. This requires humility and ongoing dialogue with the communities being represented.

Empathy enhances analysis: The project taught me that the best data work combines technical skill with genuine curiosity about human experience. Cold objectivity often misses the story the data is trying to tell.
