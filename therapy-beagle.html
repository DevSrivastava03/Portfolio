Therapy Beagle

Reimagining AI as a gentle companion

Timeline 12 weeks, Fall 2024
Role Product Designer, Researcher
Focus AI Ethics, Mental Health
Deliverables Research, Prototype, Guidelines
Therapy Beagle Interface

Overview

Designed a mindful AI experience focused on empathy, privacy, and creating space for genuine reflection. This project explores how artificial intelligence can support mental wellness while respecting emotional boundaries and maintaining user agency.

Mental health apps have exploded in popularity, yet many perpetuate extractive patterns—optimizing for engagement over wellbeing, collecting sensitive data without clear purpose, and using manipulative design to keep users dependent. Meanwhile, AI chatbots promise 24/7 emotional support but often lack the nuance and boundaries that make therapy genuinely helpful.

Therapy Beagle began with a provocation: What if we designed an AI companion that prioritized care over conversation, boundaries over engagement, and user dignity over data collection?

Design Principle

Technology should support human flourishing, not exploit vulnerability. Good design for mental health respects boundaries, maintains privacy, and empowers rather than creates dependency.

Research & Problem Space

Understanding the Landscape

I began with comprehensive research into both mental health care and AI systems:

  • Expert interviews: Spoke with 5 licensed therapists about therapeutic relationships and boundaries
  • User research: Interviewed 15 individuals using mental health apps about their experiences
  • Competitive analysis: Audited 12 mental health apps and AI chatbots for patterns and practices
  • Literature review: Examined academic research on AI ethics, mental health technology, and human-computer interaction

Key Problems Identified

01

Boundary Confusion

AI chatbots simulate intimacy without the professional boundaries that protect therapeutic relationships

02

Privacy Concerns

Users share deeply personal information without understanding data collection and usage

03

False Equivalence

Marketing presents AI as replacement for human therapy rather than complementary tool

04

Engagement Optimization

Dark patterns encourage unhealthy dependency rather than building user autonomy

Design Principles

Based on research findings, I developed five core principles to guide the design:

1. Transparency Over Simulation

Therapy Beagle never pretends to be human or to understand emotions it cannot experience. The interface clearly communicates it's an AI tool designed to support reflection, not a replacement for human connection or professional care.

2. Privacy as Default

All interactions are processed locally on-device. No conversations are stored on servers, analyzed for patterns, or used to train models. Users can export their own data but it never leaves their control.

3. Boundaries, Not Engagement

The system actively discourages overuse. Sessions have natural endpoints. There are no streaks, gamification, or notifications designed to pull users back. The goal is autonomy, not dependency.

4. Reflection Over Prescription

Rather than offering advice or solutions, Therapy Beagle asks thoughtful questions that help users develop their own insights. It functions as a mirror, not a guide.

5. Humility About Limitations

The system knows what it cannot handle. It recognizes crisis language and gently redirects to appropriate professional resources. It acknowledges that some problems require human care.

Design Solution

The Interface

The visual design deliberately avoids the slick polish common in consumer apps. Therapy Beagle feels calm, undemanding, and honest:

  • Muted color palette: Soft neutrals that reduce visual stimulation
  • Generous spacing: Interface feels unhurried and breathable
  • Clear typography: Readable without being clinical
  • Minimal decoration: No unnecessary visual elements competing for attention

Core Features

Guided Reflection Sessions

Rather than open-ended chat, users engage in structured 15-20 minute reflection sessions. The AI asks thoughtful questions tailored to the user's stated focus area (anxiety, relationships, work stress, etc.), giving space between prompts for genuine thinking.

Check-In, Not Check-Up

Daily mood tracking is optional and never framed as an obligation. The system asks "How are you feeling?" without judgment if you don't answer. There's no pressure to maintain streaks or demonstrate "progress."

Private Journaling

Users can write freely without AI analysis or response. This space is entirely theirs—no suggestions, no commentary, just blank space for unstructured thought.

Resource Library

Curated articles, exercises, and professional resources. When the AI recognizes topics beyond its scope, it suggests relevant materials or professional help rather than attempting to address them itself.

Technical Approach

Privacy-First Architecture

The technical implementation prioritizes privacy at every level:

  • Local processing: All AI inference runs on-device using TensorFlow Lite
  • No cloud storage: Conversations stored locally in encrypted format
  • Open source model: Using publicly auditable AI models, no black boxes
  • Data portability: Users can export their complete history at any time

Safeguards & Crisis Detection

The system includes pattern recognition for concerning language:

  • Detects expressions of self-harm or crisis situations
  • Immediately provides crisis hotline information
  • Gently encourages reaching out to trusted humans
  • Never attempts to "counsel" through serious mental health crises

Natural Language Processing

The conversational AI is intentionally constrained:

  • Fine-tuned on reflective questioning patterns, not general conversation
  • Avoids making declarative statements about the user's situation
  • Responds with questions that invite deeper self-exploration
  • Acknowledges uncertainty rather than fabricating confidence

Testing & Validation

User Testing

Conducted week-long trials with 12 participants who regularly use mental health apps:

  • 92% appreciated the clear boundaries and honest communication about limitations
  • 83% found the reduced engagement pressure refreshing compared to other apps
  • 100% valued the privacy-first approach and local data storage
  • 75% reported the reflection questions helped them gain new insights

Expert Review

Three licensed therapists evaluated the prototype:

  • Praised the clear distinction between AI support and professional therapy
  • Appreciated crisis detection and appropriate resource referrals
  • Noted the questioning approach aligned with reflective therapeutic practices
  • Suggested additional safeguards for vulnerable populations

Ethical Review

Consulted with AI ethics researchers and privacy advocates:

  • Validated privacy architecture and data handling practices
  • Recommended additional transparency in AI decision-making
  • Suggested clearer communication about model limitations
  • Affirmed overall alignment with responsible AI principles

Reflection & Impact

What I Learned

Ethics isn't optional: This project reinforced that ethical considerations must be embedded from the start, not applied as afterthoughts. Every design decision—from color choices to conversation patterns—carries ethical weight when dealing with mental health.

Restraint is strength: In a field dominated by feature creep and engagement optimization, saying "no" to certain capabilities proved more powerful than adding them. Sometimes the most caring design is one that does less.

Design shapes relationships: How we design AI interfaces shapes how people relate to technology. Therapy Beagle taught me that transparent, boundaried design can model healthy relationships rather than exploitative ones.

Limitations & Open Questions

  • On-device processing limits model sophistication—is this trade-off worth it?
  • How do we measure "success" for a tool designed to minimize engagement?
  • Can this approach scale while maintaining its core principles?
  • What regulations might be needed to ensure mental health AI stays ethical?

Future Directions

This project serves as a proof-of-concept for responsible AI design in mental health. Future work could explore:

  • Collaboration with mental health professionals for clinical validation
  • Accessibility features for diverse cognitive and physical abilities
  • Integration with professional therapy as a complementary tool
  • Open-sourcing the framework for others building ethical mental health tech
