Therapy Beagle

Introducing emotional slowness into digital companionship

Timeline 12 weeks, Fall 2024
Role Product Designer, Developer
Tools LLaMA 3, Ollama, Figma
Type Local-first AI Companion
Main Interface

A gentle companion that invites you to breathe, reflect, and feel — not just respond.

Late nights at Parsons, I watched friends open journaling apps only to close them seconds later.

The interfaces demanded efficiency. The prompts felt clinical. Nobody wanted another productivity tool—they wanted to feel heard.

Most AI chatbots optimize for speed and engagement metrics. Therapy Beagle challenges this by introducing emotional slowness and care into digital interactions.

User Research Notes

What I Heard

I started conversations with fellow students and creatives. Three patterns emerged:

"It feels transactional"

Existing tools treated emotional check-ins like todo lists

"I don't trust where this goes"

Nobody knew what happened to their vulnerable thoughts

"It rushes me"

Apps pushed for quick responses, but feelings don't work on a schedule

Architecture Diagram

Built for Privacy

Everything runs locally using LLaMA 3 through Ollama. No cloud servers. No data collection. Your conversations stay on your device.

This wasn't just about privacy—it fundamentally changed what the tool could be. Without engagement metrics to optimize, I could design for slowness instead of retention.

Designing for Emotional Pacing

I sketched interaction patterns that felt more like sitting with a friend than consulting a therapist. Breathing reminders before conversations. Gentle pauses between responses. No urgency to complete any task.

Typography Study

Generous line height, never cramped

"This is the first time an app didn't make me feel like I was doing emotional health wrong."

— Student tester, after using prototype for one week

Visual Language of Care

Muted blues and warm grays. Readable typography with generous line height. Subtle breathing motion during pauses. More whitespace than felt necessary—room to think, room to breathe.

Every detail was intentional. The visual design couldn't just look calm—it had to embody the slowness I wanted people to feel.

Learning Through Use

I shared prototypes with fellow students and creatives. The feedback revealed where I'd gotten it wrong—and right.

15 Students tested the prototype

Testing Session

"I actually stayed in a conversation longer than usual because it wasn't rushing me to 'insights.'"

What It Taught Me

Therapy Beagle taught me that care isn't something you add to a product—it's a foundational constraint that shapes every decision. When you commit to slowness, privacy, and emotional presence, entire categories of features become impossible.

No growth hacking. No viral loops. No data monetization. And that's exactly the point.

Final Interface in Context

Therapy Beagle remains a working prototype and a provocation. It asks: what if more digital tools were built for human flourishing instead of engagement metrics?

This project deepened my interest in exploring offline-first, privacy-centered, and emotionally aware approaches to building digital products.
